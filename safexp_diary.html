Diary of a voyage - The Safe Thrill Experience


Docking bay 2K30606R
Jita 4-4, Kimotoro, The Forge
The beating heart of New Eden.
This is where the voyage begins.

On the left, you will follow the journal of a voyager, taken during the Sightseeing on the path of Katia Sae , Safe Thrill Experience.
On the right, you will follow the host for the Sightseeing on the Path of Katia Sae experiences, Alena Eveymon.

Departure station: Jita 4-4

System: Jita
Constellation: Kimotoro
Region: The Forge
Security Status: 0.9

Ticket double checked, we are boarding. So excited!

Our host, she said she is called Alena, is rather cheerful, and the staff is very polite. I feel so pampered! They offered us a welcome drink once we settled in our seats.

I was readying from the pamphlets and the information mails that they modified the standard shuttle to give us a better view of the outside. And, even more amazing, we have a real Capsuleer pilot driving us! Albeit apparently they are rather silent and invisible.

We will be on our way soon!

"Welcome everyone, New Eden Voyages is happy to welcome you on board the NEV Voyager I.“

“My name is Alena and I'll be your host for this voyage. Be ready to be amazed and have an experience that you won't forget!" the words of the Gallentean girl were cheerful as she welcomed the guests.

“We are preparing to leave Jita 4-4 and we will be soon on our way.”

Once all the guests had boarded, the undocking operations started. The shuttle slowly rose from its berth and headed for the access corridor. The shuttle entered the flow of ships leaving the docks.

Stop one: Katia Sae Monument

System: Saisio
Constellation: Okomon
Region: The Forge
Security Status: 0.7

We are en route to the Monument to Katia Sae. The first stop.

I feel so small…

The statue is massive and golden, reflecting the light of the Saisio sun.

It’s as massive as what Katia Sae did. Visiting all systems of New Eden in 10 years… mind blowing if you think many don’t even leave their system, or worse, their planets.

It’s inspiring but also cowed. Cowed because fear I could never be that great, but at the same time I’m hopeful I can do something great, in my own dimension, at least.

Next stop Iyen-Oursta.

"To all guests, we just entered the Saisio system and we are en route to the Katia Sae Monument." the public announcement swept into the guest area and many moved to the floor to ceiling windows, eager to see the fabled monument.

"The first stop of our voyage is the Monument of Katia Sae. The Stargazer herself.” Alena’s voice drifted from the speaker “Built in YC121, this monument celebrate the achievement of this one pilot: visiting each and every system of our cluster. A massive undertaking, one that consumed many years of her life but which set her as a unique part of New Eden's history."

As Alena spoke, the shuttle moved closer to the statue. The monument filled the windows and its golden finish brought warm light into the shuttle.

"The statue depicts Katia herself as she reaches out for the stars. Close to the monument, you can see the Abagawa gate, where it all began: this is where, on December 1st YC111, she started her 10 years journey."

Her script finished, Alena remained in silent, letting the guests enjoying the sight as the ship slowly circled the monument.

Stop two: Battle of Iyen-Oursta Monument

System: Iyen-Oursta
Constellation: Gallente Border Zone
Region: Sinq Laison
Security Status: 0.8

Silence.

But what there is to say at the monument of one of the most climatic and bloody battles that this side of New Eden had seen?

Nothing.

I’m thankful for who fought at that time, and have huge respect for their sacrifice, but I hope it won’t happen again.

"Let peace prevail and nevermore war darken these stars. These are the words etched in the Monument to the battle of Iyen-Oursta. In BYC10, this system saw one of the most climatic battle in the Gallente-Caldari War, where the Gallente Federation and the Caldari State fought over the control of the system which was considered, by both parties, to be strategically important."

"However, this battle hadn't been known only for the bloodshed, but it also marked the first time capsule-controlled vessels were fielded on the battlefield by any of the four main Empires. History had changed right here."

Nobody on board the ship could have been there but many, of Gallentean or Caldari descent, had heard the tales. Some lucky ones might have met people that had been there, or in the least, people that had spoken with soldiers and seaman that had participated in the battle.

Stop three: Chribba Monument

System: Amarr
Constellation: Throne Worlds
Region: Domain
Security Status: 1.0

It feels like a joke. Truly! But apparently this Chribba guy is considered the most honest of all Capsuleers.

Impressive isn’t it?

But I guess it meant something as they built him this massive statue, in the style of Katia Sae’s one. Only that, instead of a star system, he has a little Revelation dreadnought mining holo asteroids.

We are in Amarr and everything is so golden…

"The most honest Capsuleer. A title nobody else had claimed ever since. A well-known miner in the early days of Capsuleers, Chribba went on to become a renown neutral third party in transactions between Capsuleers. Titans and supercarriers, precious and unique memorabilia, all kind of things went through the hands of this man, but not once he broke his promise to be a neutral third party. This monument is a testament to his legacy of trust and peaceful industry."

Stop four: Fallen Capsuleers Memorial

System: Molea
Constellation: Amdimmah
Region: Khanid
Security Status: 0.7

Silence.

Once again, I feel like I have no words.

We are at the Fallen Capsuleer Memorial, which is a graveyard. This golden pod held up by golden statues on top of a forever burning light and three black arching monolith.

It’s strange: we always see Capsuleers as immortals and yet, they built a Memorial for those of them who passed away.

They can die.

Immortality is thus… not immortal?

Sobering.

"A graveyard. A cathedral of mourning. This is what the Fallen Capsuleer Memorial is. Here, are gathered the spiritual remains of all the capsuleers that have passed, because yes, not all capsuleers are forever immortal."

"Originally built by capsuleers in YC109 in Kor-Azor, it was moved to this location at a later time. However, at the start it wasn't as impressive as it relied on player owned stations to be. Only in YC122, the Upwell Consortium stepped in and built the monument you see now."

Stop five: Project Discovery Phase One Monument

System: Lanngisi
Constellation: Ani
Region: Metropolis
Security Status: 0.5

I love science and I had participated in Project Discovery so seeing my efforts somewhat recognized in a monument… it makes me proud!

We are at the Project Discovery Monument, celebrating the eponymous project of Citizen Science.

These projects can really help science move forward. There should be more of these.

"The Citizen Science project. This is what Project Discovery is and this monument celebrates its first Phase."

"Launched by the Sisters of EvE in YC118, Capsuleers and normal citizens alike had been able to make science progress by helping scientists all over the cluster catalogue a vast amount of stellar signatures and genetic samples."

Arrival station: Jita 4-4

System: Jita
Constellation: Kimotoro
Region: The Forge
Security Status: 0.9

And it’s ending… already?! Ah!

Yes, we are in sight of Jita 4-4. Seen from outside the station is even more impressive: it’s massive but even more massive is the traffic. So many ships, from small shuttles to massive Capsuleers’ cargo ships. I hope one of those doesn’t crush us by mistake!

The docking operations had been rather smooth but that means this voyage, as they call it, has come to an end.

I’m sad… cause it’s ended.

What to say: the sights are oh so worth it. You’re pampered as it’s clearly a first class service and Alena, the host, actually seems to know what she is talking about and not repeating something she had learned by memory, like in other tours I took.

Would I do it again? I’d say yes.

However, right now… I’m tempted by the Mild Thrill experience. I have to give it a thought.

Until then,
See you.

~The Voyager
